Q1

Suppose BB acquires one of the local breweries through a buyout. Let's denote that acquired local brewery as BB2. This buyout may result in higher prices for some brew in some counties but lower prices in other counties. For counties that the local brewery was avaialbe, consumers experienced the disutility because the local brewery is related to the corporate brand. So the demand in the original home market decrease for the local brewery. Instead, the share for other breweries increase so that the prices of other breweries increases. For counties that the local brewery was not avaible, the new coming brewery will take the market share from the orginal available brewery so that the prices for the original breweries decrease.

Q2

Yes, the home market price dropped because of the disutility of buyout. Even thought the product characteristics doesn't change, consumers still have lower utility.

Q3

Suppose BB2 adopted BB's cost structure, for the counties that BB2 was available, the lower cost structure would drop the price more; For the counties that BB2 is new, the lower cost structure would still lead to price increasing, but not the same as the case that cost structure doesn't change.

#use old price to generate new price
def new_price(Choice_set, dummies, p_initial, ownership, index, s_temp, I):
    s_all_id=[]
    s_all_id=[id_share(Choice_set, dummies, p_initial, index, s_temp) for i in range(I)]
    # s_all_id[0][0][0]
    
    #market share
    s_initial=[]
    s_initial=[[sum(s_all_id[i][t][j] for i in range(I))/I for j in range(len(Choice_set[t]))] for t in range(len(Choice_set))]

    #generate ds/dp matrix
    ds=[np.zeros((len(Choice_set[t]),len(Choice_set[t]))) for t in range(len(Choice_set))]
    for t in range(len(Choice_set)):
        for j in range(len(Choice_set[t])):
            for k in range(len(Choice_set[t])):
                if (j==k):
                    ds[t][j][k]=sum(s_all_id[i][t][k]*s_all_id[i][t][j]-s_all_id[i][t][k] for i in range(I))/I
                if (j!=k):
                    ds[t][j][k]=sum(s_all_id[i][t][k]*s_all_id[i][t][j] for i in range(I))/I

    #multiply ownership matrix and ds/dp matrix element  by element
    dsdp=[np.zeros((len(Choice_set[t]),len(Choice_set[t]))) for t in range(len(Choice_set))]
    dsdp=[[[ownership[t][i][j]*ds[t][i][j] for j in range(len(Choice_set[t]))] for i in range(len(Choice_set[t]))] for t in range(len(Choice_set))]

    # dsdp_inverse=[np.zeros((len(Choice_set[t]),len(Choice_set[t]))) for t in range(len(Choice_set))]
    # for t in range(len(Choice_set)):
    #     for i in range(len(Choice_set[t])):
    #         for j in range(len(Choice_set[t])):
    #             if dsdp[t][i][j]!=0:
    #                 dsdp_inverse[t][i][j]=dsdp[t][i][j]**(-1)
    dsdp_inverse=[]
    dsdp_inverse=[np.linalg.inv(np.array(dsdp[t])) for t in range(len(Choice_set))]

    #generate p_new
    omega=[0]*len(Choice_set)
    w=[0]*len(Choice_set)
    mc=[0]*len(Choice_set)
    for i in range(len(Choice_set)):
        omega[i] = [0] * len(Choice_set[i])
        w[i] = [0] * len(Choice_set[i])
        mc[i] = [0] * len(Choice_set[i])

    for t in range(len(Choice_set)):
        for j in range(len(Choice_set[t])):
            omega[t][j]=np.exp(np.random.normal(0,1,1)/10)
            w[t][j]=np.exp(np.random.normal(0,1,1)/8)
            if (index[t][j]==0):
                mc[t][j]=0
            if (index[t][j]<6 and index[t][j]>0):
                mc[t][j]=omega[t][j]*w[t][j]
            elif (index[t][j]>=6):
                mc[t][j]=omega[t][j]*w[t][j]*0.85
    # mc=[[np.exp(np.random.normal(0,1,1)/8)*np.exp(np.random.normal(0,1,1)/10) for j in range(len(Choice_set[t]))] for t in range(len(Choice_set))]
    # p=[mc[t]-np.matmul(np.array(dsdp_inverse[t]),np.array(s_initial[t])) for t in range(len(Choice_set))]
    p=[0]*len(Choice_set)
    for i in range(len(Choice_set)):
        p[i] = [0] * len(Choice_set[i])

    for t in range(len(Choice_set)):
        a=np.matmul(np.array(dsdp_inverse[t]),np.array(s_initial[t]))
        for j in range(len(Choice_set[t])):
            p[t][j]=mc[t][j]-a[j]
    return p, s_initial, mc, dsdp, w

After generating the price and market share, we use nested logit model to estimate $\beta$, $\alpha$ and $\sigma$. \begin{align} log(s_{jt}-log(s_{0t}))=x_j\beta+\alpha p_jt+\sigma log(s_{jt|ht})+\epsilon_{jt} \end{align}

In particular, I dropped the outside good sample so that there are 197 samples in total. Since price and group share are endogeneous varibles, so I use two instrument variables. The first one is the own cost shifter $w_{jt}$ and the another one is the sum of the cost shifter for the breweries other than product j $\sum_{-j}w_{-jt}$.

The nested logit model assume that the own price derivatives are negative while the other cross price derivatives are positive which means that if the firm owns two products, these two are substitutes, but it may not be. $$ \frac{ds_{jt}}{dp_{kt}}=\left\{ \begin{aligned} & = -\alpha s_{jt}(\frac{1}{1-\sigma}-\frac{\sigma}{1-\sigma}s_{jt|g}-s_{jt}), if \quad j=k \\ & = \alpha s_{kt}(\frac{\sigma}{1-\sigma}s_{jt|g}+s_{jt}), if \quad j\quad k \quad in \quad the \quad same \quad nest \\ & = \alpha s_{jt} s_{kt}, otherwise \end{aligned} \right. $$

def new_price_p1(Choice_set, dummies, p_initial, ownership, index, s_temp, I):
    s_all_id=[]
    s_all_id=[id_share_p(Choice_set, dummies, p_initial, index, s_temp) for i in range(I)]
    # s_all_id[0][0][0]
    
    #market share
    s_initial=[]
    s_initial=[[sum(s_all_id[i][t][j] for i in range(I))/I for j in range(len(Choice_set[t]))] for t in range(len(Choice_set))]

    #generate ds/dp matrix
    ds=[np.zeros((len(Choice_set[t]),len(Choice_set[t]))) for t in range(len(Choice_set))]
    for t in range(len(Choice_set)):
        for j in range(len(Choice_set[t])):
            for k in range(len(Choice_set[t])):
                if (j==k):
                    ds[t][j][k]=sum(s_all_id[i][t][k]*s_all_id[i][t][j]-s_all_id[i][t][k] for i in range(I))/I
                if (j!=k):
                    ds[t][j][k]=sum(s_all_id[i][t][k]*s_all_id[i][t][j] for i in range(I))/I

    #multiply ownership matrix and ds/dp matrix element  by element
    dsdp=[np.zeros((len(Choice_set[t]),len(Choice_set[t]))) for t in range(len(Choice_set))]
    dsdp=[[[ownership[t][i][j]*ds[t][i][j] for j in range(len(Choice_set[t]))] for i in range(len(Choice_set[t]))] for t in range(len(Choice_set))]

    # dsdp_inverse=[np.zeros((len(Choice_set[t]),len(Choice_set[t]))) for t in range(len(Choice_set))]
    # for t in range(len(Choice_set)):
    #     for i in range(len(Choice_set[t])):
    #         for j in range(len(Choice_set[t])):
    #             if dsdp[t][i][j]!=0:
    #                 dsdp_inverse[t][i][j]=dsdp[t][i][j]**(-1)
    dsdp_inverse=[]
    dsdp_inverse=[np.linalg.inv(np.array(dsdp[t])) for t in range(len(Choice_set))]

    #generate p_new
    omega=[0]*len(Choice_set)
    w=[0]*len(Choice_set)
    mc=[0]*len(Choice_set)
    for i in range(len(Choice_set)):
        omega[i] = [0] * len(Choice_set[i])
        w[i] = [0] * len(Choice_set[i])
        mc[i] = [0] * len(Choice_set[i])

    for t in range(len(Choice_set)):
        for j in range(len(Choice_set[t])):
            omega[t][j]=np.exp(np.random.normal(0,1,1)/10)
            w[t][j]=np.exp(np.random.normal(0,1,1)/8)
            if (index[t][j]<5 and index[t][j>0]):
                mc[t][j]=omega[t][j]*w[t][j]
            if (index[t][j]>=5 and index[t][j]<10):
                mc[t][j]=omega[t][j]*w[t][j]*0.85
            if (index[t][j]==0):
                mc[t][j]=0
            elif (index[t][j]==10):
                mc[t][j]=omega[t][j]*w[t][j]
    # mc=[[np.exp(np.random.normal(0,1,1)/8)*np.exp(np.random.normal(0,1,1)/10) for j in range(len(Choice_set[t]))] for t in range(len(Choice_set))]
    # p=[mc[t]-np.matmul(np.array(dsdp_inverse[t]),np.array(s_initial[t])) for t in range(len(Choice_set))]
    p=[0]*len(Choice_set)
    for i in range(len(Choice_set)):
        p[i] = [0] * len(Choice_set[i])

    for t in range(len(Choice_set)):
        a=np.matmul(np.array(dsdp_inverse[t]),np.array(s_initial[t]))
        for j in range(len(Choice_set[t])):
            p[t][j]=mc[t][j]-a[j]
    return p, s_initial, mc, dsdp, w
